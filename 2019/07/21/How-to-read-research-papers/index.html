Career advice / Reading Research Papers

Read research papers

Compile list of papers (or medium/blog posts)

Read 1 paper:

Multiple passes

  1. Title/abstract/figures
  2. Intro + Conclusions + Figures + skim rest (skip related work)
  3. Read but skip/skim math
  4. Whole thing, but skip parts hard to understand

Questions to ask:

What did the authors try to accomplish?

What were the key elements of the approach?

What can you use yourself?

What other references do you want to follow?

Sources of paper:

Twitter

ML subreddit

NIPS/ICML/ICLR

Arxiv sanity

Math:

Read through it, take detailed notes

Rederive the math from scratch

Code:

Download open-source code

Reimplement from scratch

Steady reading rather than short-term burst.


Career Advice

Goal:

Job(big company or startup)

PhD.

Whatever the choice is, the goal is to do important work(I hope).

How to get a position:

Recruiters(or admission committees) look for:

Skills(ML quiz, coding)

Having done meaningful work (can do work)

Broad understanding in the areas of ML, and have deep understanding in at least one of the areas.


Improve foundational skills

Course work

Reading research papers

Delve deep into one area

Relevant project


Saturday morning problem:

Read a paper on research

Work on research

Work on open-source

…

It’s not about doing this hard for just a week end but the most important thing is to this consistently.


Selecting a position:

Work with great people and projects

Focus on the team(10 - 30 persons) you interact with

Manager

Not on “brand”

Find a place where you can learn the most and do important work.
