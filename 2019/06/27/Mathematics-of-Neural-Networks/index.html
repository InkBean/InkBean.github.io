Each iteration over all the training examples basically does four steps

  1. Forward propagation
  2. Compute cost function
  3. Backward propagation
  4. Update parameters (using parameters, and grads from backprop)

Forward propagation

To fully understand the process of forward propagation, we need to answer ourselves two questions.

​ 1. What are the dimensions of the matrices?

​ 2. Why do they have such dimensions? (How to interpret their dimensions)

Let’s start from the input.

dim of X = (nx, m) = (n[0], m)

We know that each training example of X has nx (or n[o] since it’s at the 0th layer) # of features, and we have m training examples.

The matrix X is made by stacking up m training examples column by column, each column stands for one training example of X, of dimension(n[0], 1). Keep in mind that each training example of X is a column vector.


To compute the values of the nodes of the 1st layer,

we need two more parameters: W[1] and b[1]

dim of W[1] = (n[1], n[0])

dim of b[1] = (n[1], 1)

and two steps of calculation.

  1. Z[1] = W[1] * X + b

  2. A[1] = g(Z[1])

dim of Z[1] = (n[1], m)

dim of A[1] = (n[1], m)
